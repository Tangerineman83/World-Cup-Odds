How it works

Each team's current Elo rating is converted to win/draw/loss probabilities for every possible match (home advantage +100 Elo for co-hosts USA, Canada, Mexico). The full 104-match tournament is simulated 20,000 times; the table shows how often each team reaches each stage.

What's simplified

The Round-of-32 placement of the 8 best third-placed teams uses an approximate mapping, not FIFA's official 495-scenario "Annex C" table. Results already played are not yet factored in — this is a pre-tournament-style baseline.

Reading the columns

R32 / R16 / QF / SF / F / Champion are cumulative probabilities of reaching that stage (Champion = winning the final). 1st / 2nd are probabilities of finishing top or second in the group.

Methodology. Match outcomes are drawn from the eloratings.net expected-result formula (dr = rating difference, +100 for the home/host side; We = 1 / (10^(−dr/400) + 1)), split into win/draw/loss probabilities via an empirical draw model (≈26% draw probability for evenly matched teams, falling to a floor of ≈12% for lopsided ones). Knockout draws are resolved via extra time/penalties, modelled as roughly a coin flip with a small tilt toward the higher-rated side. Group-stage goal differences (used for tiebreaks) are sampled from Poisson distributions calibrated to each match's win probability.

Co-host nations (USA, Canada, Mexico) receive the +100 home-advantage adjustment in every match they play, including knockout rounds — this is a deliberate modelling choice (consistent with the eloratings.net convention) and is one reason host-nation tournament-win probabilities here may sit higher than you'd see in betting/prediction markets.

This is an independent, simplified simulation for illustrative purposes — not an official forecast. Regenerate via node scripts/sim/runSimulation.js and commit the updated predictions.json.
