Knockout bracket

How this scenario is built. For each group, 5,000 simulations are run (using the Elo-based win/draw/loss model with the +100 home-advantage convention for co-hosts USA, Canada, and Mexico); the most frequently occurring full 1st-4th order is used as that group's "predicted" result. The eight best third-placed teams are then ranked (using Elo as a proxy for points/goal difference - an approximation of the official process) and placed into the Round of 32 using a simplified bracket mapping. From the Round of 32 onward, the higher-rated team (including penalty-shootout odds for drawn knockout matches) is taken as the winner at each stage.

This is one representative scenario, not a forecast with a stated probability of occurring in full - the chance of every single result going exactly this way is small. For the underlying per-team, per-stage probabilities (e.g. "Brazil's chance of reaching the semi-final"), see the full probability table.
