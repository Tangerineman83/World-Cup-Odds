How we work this out. Every team has a rating based on their recent results — the better a team's results, the higher their rating. We use these ratings to work out the chance of each team winning any match, including a boost for the three host nations (USA, Canada and Mexico) when they play. We then run the whole tournament thousands of times on a computer to see how often each team wins their group, reaches the knockout rounds, and lifts the trophy.

What you see here is one likely path through the tournament — not a guarantee, and not the only way things could go. For the full picture — every team's chance of reaching each stage — see the full table.

Results already played are built in and update each team's rating as the tournament goes on, so this page should get more accurate week by week. We give extra weight to what happens at this World Cup itself — a good or bad result here moves a team's rating more than the same result would outside the tournament, since it's the freshest evidence of how a team is actually playing right now.
